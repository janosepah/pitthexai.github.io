Openings

Interested applicants with a strong command of AI and health data science, and particularly medical image analysis may apply. We are always looking for self-motivated students, postdocs, and visiting scholars to join Pitt HexAI Research Laboratory.

If you are an Undergraduate or Master student at the University of Pittsburgh, and mainly interested in fundamental or/and applied AI algorithms in healthcare research, please email Prof. Tafti your CV and a brief description of your research interests.

If you are interested in joing the Pitt HexAI Research Laboratory as a PhD student, then please email Prof. Tafti your CV, a description of your research interests, plus bachelor/master transcripts (in case you are not at the University of Pittsburgh yet). We are recruiting PhD students from several graduate programs, including SHRS, School of Computing and Information, Intelligent Systems Program, and other programs at Pitt.

If you are interested in joing our research laboratory as a visiting scholar, please email Prof. Tafti your CV, a letter of research interests, and funding sources.

For your information, We are now receiving several emails in a weekly-basis, if you do not hear from us, then that somehow means we won't like to move forward. We are really sorry! Also, all information/CV you send us will be kept private.

Potential Benefits

Potential benefits to join HexAI Research Laboratory include but not limited to:

  • Working with world-class leaders in health data science research and a multidisciplinary team of computer scientists, physicians, surgeons, health informaticians, and statisticians.
  • Developing mentoring relationships with faculty member(s) and scientists.
  • Working on real-world, clinically significant, and highly practical AI applications.
  • Networking with professional communities in the healthcare domain and leveraging real clinical data.
  • Participating in educational/training opportunities provided frequently by the research laboratory.
  • Access to high-performance computational resources.
